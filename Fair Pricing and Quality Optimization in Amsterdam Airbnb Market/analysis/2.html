Here I gathered data from Amsterdam Open Data Portal, Open Map street, Airbnb, and other resources for housing internal characteristic features and external service, amenity and in-facility features and geographical boundary information for neighborhood effect consideration. These features will form an inventory for subsequent process for different models and different subjects. I also gathered data that relating to different development context such as whether in a development area or poor area to make further comparison analysis and validation. Unrelated, invalid, false-typed and biased data are filtered out initially through common sense.

The data can be divided into 3 types, and each one is listed blow with their detailed information. Significantly, this inventory is the basic foundation, the variable library will be cleaned, updated and stated with modeling description.

1.2 Internal Inventory

First, I deconstruct the basic check in data to obtain internal features for every order, such as the date of the order real-time housing price. Then through extraction and processing, I obtained the month, the day, and the number of orders of each housing units, the real-time price (not the recommended or average price), and the total revenue the housing generated. It is a large list including 1,200,071 objects and could be used for price prediction for renters. Here, I fully excavate extensive possible internal features from text analysis and matching. There are more than 100 internal amenity key words, and host information, room numbers, availability, renting rules, order information, and evaluations. Many variables here can be the independent variables according to different models and serving subjects. In my data pool, mutiple boolean variables showing whether they have those amenities.
